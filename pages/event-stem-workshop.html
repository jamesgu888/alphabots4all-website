November 28, 2024

Community STEM Workshop Success

Workshop Community Education

We hosted a hands-on robotics workshop for local middle school students, introducing them to programming and mechanical design. Over 50 students participated in building their first robots!

Workshop Activities:

  • Basic robot construction with LEGO Mindstorms
  • Introduction to block-based programming
  • Sensor integration and testing
  • Mini competition with obstacle courses

The excitement in the room was palpable as students saw their creations come to life. Many participants expressed interest in joining our mentorship program.

Special thanks to our volunteer mentors who made this event possible and to the local schools that helped us reach these aspiring young engineers.

Student Feedback:

"I never thought I could build a robot that actually moves! This workshop made me want to learn more about engineering." - Sarah, 7th grade
"The programming part was challenging but fun. I want to join the robotics club at my school now!" - Miguel, 8th grade

The workshop concluded with a mini-tournament where teams navigated their robots through obstacle courses. The competitive spirit and collaborative problem-solving we witnessed gives us great hope for the future of STEM education.

Looking Forward:

Based on the success of this workshop, we're planning to expand our community outreach program with monthly workshops and the establishment of robotics clubs in partner schools.
